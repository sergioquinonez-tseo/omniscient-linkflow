← Distribution & leaderboard

1 · Starved priorities vs equity sinks

Every page by priority tier (left = most important) and flow share (log). Bubble size = organic traffic. The pages that decide the link plan live in two zones: starved priorities (tier 1-2, below the tier-2 median flow) are link targets; equity sinks (tier 3-4, in the top decile of flow) are link sources.

2 · Cluster flow share

Where the site's flow sits by topic cluster. In compare mode the inner ring is the baseline run.

3 · Hub health

Share of each cluster's own flow that sits on its designated hub. A healthy hub-and-spoke cluster concentrates a meaningful share on the hub; near-zero bars mean the cluster has authority but the citable page does not.

4 · Concentration curve

Cumulative share of total flow held by the top N% of pages. The further the curve bows from the diagonal, the more concentrated the site (Gini). Compare mode overlays the baseline as a dashed line.

6 · Flow by click depth

Clicks from the crawl entry page. AI crawlers discover and refresh pages by following links; pages beyond ~3 clicks are discovered less reliably. Bars: page count; line: share of total flow at that depth.

5 · Equity map (treemap)

Clusters sized by total flow; pages within each cluster sized by their share, colored by tier (dark olive = tier 1, gray = tier 4). Big gray/light blocks inside a cluster are hoarding spokes; tiny dark blocks are starved priority pages. Click a cluster to zoom.
